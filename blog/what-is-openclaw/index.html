What is OpenClaw? The Complete Guide for Business Owners

You've probably heard about AI assistants like ChatGPT and Claude. But what if you could have an AI assistant that runs on your server, connects to the messaging apps your team already uses, and automates workflows around the clock ‚Äî all while keeping your data completely private?

That's OpenClaw. And this guide covers everything you need to know about it as a business owner.

What is OpenClaw?

OpenClaw is a self-hosted, open-source AI assistant that acts as a gateway between your messaging apps and AI models like Claude, GPT-4, Llama, and others. You install it on your own server (or VPS), and it becomes an always-on AI assistant accessible from WhatsApp, Slack, Discord, Telegram, iMessage, Microsoft Teams, Google Chat, Signal, and more ‚Äî all simultaneously.

Think of it this way: instead of opening a separate AI app, you message your AI assistant directly in the apps you already use. It reads your message, processes it through whatever AI model you choose, and responds right there in the conversation.

But OpenClaw goes far beyond just answering questions. It can:

  • Run automated workflows on a schedule (research, reports, email triage)
  • Monitor channels and respond proactively to team questions
  • Execute custom skills that connect to your specific tools and APIs
  • Control a web browser, file system, and external services
  • Manage multiple agents with different roles and permissions

It's MIT-licensed, community-driven, and completely free to use. You only pay for hosting and AI API costs.

How Does OpenClaw Work?

OpenClaw runs a single process called the Gateway on your server. The Gateway is the control plane ‚Äî it manages sessions, routes messages, connects channels, and orchestrates everything.

The Architecture

Here's the flow in simple terms:

  1. You send a message from any connected app (WhatsApp, Slack, etc.)
  2. The Gateway receives it and routes it to the right AI agent
  3. The agent processes it using your chosen AI model (Claude, GPT-4, etc.)
  4. The response comes back through the same channel

The Gateway also provides a Web Control UI ‚Äî a browser dashboard where you can chat with your agent, manage configuration, view sessions, and pair devices. You can access it locally or remotely via Tailscale.

Key Technical Components

  • Gateway: The core process ‚Äî handles routing, sessions, channels, cron jobs, and webhooks
  • Channels: Plugins for each messaging platform (WhatsApp, Slack, Discord, etc.)
  • Skills: Custom capabilities you add ‚Äî from simple automations to complex workflows
  • Nodes: Companion apps for macOS, iOS, and Android that pair with your Gateway
  • Canvas: A live visual workspace the agent can render and control

Frequently Asked Questions

Is OpenClaw safe to use with sensitive business data?

Yes. Because OpenClaw is self-hosted, your data stays on your server. The only external calls are to the AI model provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, etc.) for processing ‚Äî and those are direct API calls from your server, not routed through a middleman. You control logging, retention, and access.

Can I use OpenClaw without technical knowledge?

Basic terminal comfort is helpful for DIY setup, but the onboarding wizard handles most of the configuration. Many business owners work with a setup partner (like us) to get started, then manage day-to-day usage through familiar messaging apps.

Does OpenClaw replace my existing tools?

No ‚Äî it enhances them. OpenClaw integrates with your existing messaging platforms, calendars, email, and business tools. It's the AI layer that sits on top of your current stack.

How many people can use one OpenClaw instance?

There's no user limit. One Gateway instance can serve your entire team across all connected channels. The only scaling consideration is the VPS resources, which handle typical small-to-medium business usage easily on a $5‚Äì10/month server.

What happens if my server goes down?

OpenClaw includes a daemon service (systemd on Linux, launchd on macOS) that automatically restarts the Gateway if it crashes. For production use, standard VPS monitoring and backups apply ‚Äî the same practices you'd use for any business-critical service.
